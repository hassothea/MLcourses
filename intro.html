Intro

Consider the following examples of emails.

Data

We have a collection of emails. One can represent it as follow:

  • Input \(X\): bag of words (vector of word frequencies).
  • label \(y\): type of emails (spam or nonspam).

\[\begin{align} X=\left(\begin{array} \ x_{11} & x_{12} & \dots & x_{1d}\\ x_{21} & x_{22} & \dots & x_{2d}\\ \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \vdots\\ x_{n1} & x_{n2} & \dots & x_{nd} \end{array}\right), \qquad y=\left(\begin{array} \ y_{1}\\ y_{2}\\ \vdots\\ y_{n} \end{array}\right)\ \text{with }y_i\in\{\text{spam, nonspam}\}, \forall i=1,...,n. \end{align}\]
